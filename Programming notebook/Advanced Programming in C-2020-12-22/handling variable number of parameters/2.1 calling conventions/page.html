- Caller or Invoker is a function which calls another function. This is responsible for preparing arguments and receiving the result.
- Callee or Invokee is a function being called. This is responsible for intercepting arguments, associating them with parameters, and preparing and returning the result.
- The way in which caller and callee communicates is called the calling convention.

A separate part of the memory named stack is used to transfer argumment values from caller to callee. Processor provides at least two instructions named push and pop to put a value on a stack and to get a value from the stack.

The caller pushes the argument on the stack, the callee makes use of them, and someone removes them from the stac.

The arguments may be pushed in the order of the passed arguments and the callee may be responsible for cleaning the stacks. This convention is called Pascal and avoided in modern C compilers.

The arguments may be pushed in the order of the passed arguments and the caller may be responsible for cleaning the stacks. This convention is called stdcall.

The arguments may be pushed in the opposite order of the passed arguments and the caller may be responsible for cleaning the stack. This convention is called cdecl.
